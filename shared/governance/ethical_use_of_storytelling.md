# Ethical Use of Storytelling

Storytelling can humanize, preserve memory, and communicate meaning. It can also manipulate, erase, stereotype, or justify harm.

## Guardrails

- Ask whose story is being told.
- Ask who has authority to tell it.
- Preserve cultural and historical specificity.
- Avoid imposing universal models mechanically.
- Distinguish compelling narrative from truthful representation.
- Do not exploit trauma for emotional effect.
- Track narrative risk in political and institutional storytelling.
- Treat oral traditions as living transmission, not raw material.
