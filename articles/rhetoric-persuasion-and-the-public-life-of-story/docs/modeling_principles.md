# Modeling Principles

## Public stories persuade

Stories can justify, legitimate, mobilize, accuse, defend, warn, reconcile, exclude, or invite action.

## Persuasion is not automatically manipulation

Public reasoning requires persuasion, but persuasion must be accountable to evidence, context, dignity, and audience judgment.

## Ethos, pathos, and logos interact

Credibility, emotion, and reasoning work together in public story.

## Identification must be governed

Persuasion often works by inviting audiences into a shared identity. This can support solidarity or produce exclusion.

## Computation supports review

Scores reveal rhetorical structure and review priorities. They do not determine truth, ethics, or legitimacy.
