# Modeling Principles

## Storytelling is interpretive

Models can audit patterns, but they cannot determine meaning without human interpretation.

## Structure is not formula

A structure reveals relationships among events, actors, conflicts, values, and transformations. A formula imposes a fixed pattern.

## Narrative analysis needs context

Storytelling analysis should record teller, audience, medium, cultural setting, evidence, power, and ethical stakes.

## Scores are governance aids

Coherence, craft, and risk scores help prioritize review. They do not decide whether a story is true, good, ethical, or meaningful.

## Ethical review is required

Stories can persuade, humanize, simplify, distort, erase, or harm. Representation and evidence checks are part of responsible narrative work.
