# Modeling Principles

## Safeguard practice, not only artifacts

A transcript, recording, or archive item cannot fully preserve the living storytelling system.

## Transmission matters

Safeguarding should support elders, practitioners, learners, language vitality, apprenticeship, and repeated performance.

## Consent is layered

Permission to listen is not permission to record, publish, translate, adapt, monetize, or use as data.

## Access must be governed

Some storytelling materials are public. Others are community-only, restricted, private, sacred, seasonal, or not to be archived.

## Archives should support living continuity

Documentation is useful when it strengthens community control, context, and transmission.
