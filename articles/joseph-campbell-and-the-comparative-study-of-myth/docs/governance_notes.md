# Governance Notes

Review comparative myth claims when:
- universal claim strength is high
- counterexample inclusion is weak
- context loss is high
- formula reduction is high
- cultural specificity is weak
- source tradition is unclear
- ritual context is missing
- community authority is missing
- ethical risk is high
- public consequence is high

Governance outputs should document:
- review owner
- claim context
- comparative pattern
- cultural specificity
- generalization risk
- interpretation readiness
- revision recommendation
