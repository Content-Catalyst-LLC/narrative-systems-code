# Modeling Principles

## Campbell is a lens, not a law

Use Campbell to generate questions about symbolic pattern, not to force every story into one path.

## Comparison must preserve difference

Comparative mythology is strongest when it asks what is similar and what remains non-equivalent.

## Context comes first

Language, ritual, historical context, source tradition, oral performance, and community authority should be documented before pattern matching.

## Counterexamples matter

Stories that do not fit the hero's journey are evidence, not failures.

## Formula drift is a risk

Symbolic interpretation becomes weaker when turned into a mandatory writing template.
