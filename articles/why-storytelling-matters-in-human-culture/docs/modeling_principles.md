# Modeling Principles

## Cultural story function is plural

Stories may preserve memory, teach conduct, form identity, create belonging, support moral imagination, coordinate groups, or shape public action.

## Transmission is not only textual

Stories move through speech, ritual, song, image, performance, archive, platform, and repetition.

## Cultural context matters

A story's function cannot be interpreted responsibly without context, tradition, medium, teller, audience, and use.

## Narrative risk is governance-relevant

High persuasive intensity, low source transparency, low representation care, and high audience consequence should trigger review.

## Computation supports interpretation

Scores help prioritize review and reveal assumptions. They do not determine cultural meaning.
