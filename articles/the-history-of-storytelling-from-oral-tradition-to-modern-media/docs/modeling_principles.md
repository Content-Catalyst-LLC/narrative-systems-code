# Modeling Principles

## Story media change narrative conditions

Each medium changes memory, preservation, authority, circulation, participation, access, and governance.

## Media history is layered

New media rarely erase older forms. Oral, textual, visual, broadcast, and digital practices overlap and recombine.

## Preservation is not neutral

Archives, institutions, platforms, collectors, translators, and publishers shape which stories survive and how they are understood.

## Participation varies by system

Audiences participate through listening, response, performance, interpretation, remix, choice, and community circulation.

## Computation supports interpretation

Scores help compare media affordances and preservation risks. They do not determine cultural value or historical meaning.
