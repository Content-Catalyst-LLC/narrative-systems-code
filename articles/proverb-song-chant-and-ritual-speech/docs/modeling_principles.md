# Modeling Principles

## Compact forms are not minor forms

Shortness does not mean thinness. Proverbs, songs, chants, and ritual speech can carry dense social, sacred, historical, and political meaning.

## Sound matters

Rhythm, melody, cadence, refrain, pause, and participation often carry meaning that text alone cannot preserve.

## Use context matters

The same saying or song may do different work depending on speaker, audience, place, occasion, and authority.

## Ritual speech may act

Blessings, vows, names, invocations, laments, and ceremonial formulas can change social reality.

## Governance is required

Access, consent, translation, recording, quotation, sampling, and adaptation require review.
