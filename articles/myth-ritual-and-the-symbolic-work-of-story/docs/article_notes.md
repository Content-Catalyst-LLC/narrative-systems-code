# Article Notes: Myth, Ritual, and the Symbolic Work of Story

This article examines myth and ritual as linked systems of story, symbol, action, memory, authority, and embodied meaning.

Core themes:
- myth as symbolic narrative, not merely falsehood
- ritual as meaningful repetition and embodied action
- symbolic work of story
- origin, order, cosmos, and obligation
- liminality, threshold, and transformation
- sacrifice, renewal, and repair
- myth, ritual, and power
- psychological and symbolic interpretation
- modern myths and public rituals
- ethical risk, appropriation, scapegoating, and totalization
