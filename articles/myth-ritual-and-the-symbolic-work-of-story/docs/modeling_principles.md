# Modeling Principles

## Myth is symbolic narrative

Myth should not be reduced to falsehood. It often organizes origin, order, memory, identity, authority, and ritual practice.

## Ritual is embodied meaning

Ritual can enact meaning through action, gesture, place, object, sequence, repetition, song, chant, silence, and participation.

## Symbols carry multiple meanings

Symbolic interpretation should avoid single-code decoding. Symbols gather meanings across context, tradition, history, psychology, and ritual use.

## Power must be analyzed

Myth and ritual can legitimize authority, produce belonging, exclude outsiders, or stage resistance.

## Ethical risk must be visible

Totalizing myth, scapegoating, exclusion, appropriation, and harm exposure require governance review.
