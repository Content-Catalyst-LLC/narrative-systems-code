# Modeling Principles

## Beginnings frame attention

A beginning establishes where the audience enters, what they notice, what pressure matters, and what kind of promise is made.

## Endings interpret the whole

An ending reorganizes prior meaning by resolving, transforming, suspending, returning, opening, or exposing consequence.

## Closure is not resolution

Closure is a felt stopping point. Resolution answers a pressure or question. Completion describes formal wholeness.

## Open endings must be earned

Incompletion works when unresolved consequence is meaningful, not when the story avoids responsibility.

## Closure requires governance

Closure can create false repair, sentimental comfort, system flattening, or premature resolution.
