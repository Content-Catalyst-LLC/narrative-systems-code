# Legal Narrative Responsibility Review Checklist

- Is the forum and procedural posture clearly identified?
- Is evidence separated from allegation, inference, and argument?
- Are relevance, authentication, provenance, and corroboration visible?
- Is the applicable standard of proof stated?
- Are witness memory, trauma, translation, and cross-examination context handled carefully?
- Does the story preserve uncertainty?
- Does the story flatten causation or institutional responsibility?
- Are legal records and archives described with their limits?
- Has AI-generated legal text been checked for hallucinated authority?
- Is human legal review documented?
