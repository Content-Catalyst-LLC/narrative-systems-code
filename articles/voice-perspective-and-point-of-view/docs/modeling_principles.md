# Modeling Principles

## Voice is address

Voice concerns the manner and authority of telling, including tone, diction, rhythm, address, judgment, and rhetorical habit.

## Perspective is angle

Perspective concerns the perceptual, emotional, interpretive, or ideological angle through which the story is filtered.

## Point of view is access

Point of view governs what the audience can know, when they can know it, and from what structural position.

## Reliability is graded

Reliability is not binary. A voice may be reliable about events, unreliable about motive, reliable about memory feeling, and unreliable about interpretation.

## Access requires governance

Narrative access distributes dignity, authority, exposure, sympathy, and responsibility. Perspective is ethical as well as technical.
